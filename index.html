The Eiffel Tower

The Eiffel Tower is one of the most famous landmarks in the world and a symbol of France. Located in Paris, the tower was designed by engineer Gustave Eiffel and completed in 1889 for the World's Fair. Standing at a height of approximately 330 meters, it was once the tallest man-made structure on Earth.

Every year, millions of tourists visit the Eiffel Tower to enjoy its breathtaking views, beautiful lighting, and historical importance. The tower has three levels open to visitors, with restaurants, observation decks, and souvenir shops available inside.

At night, the Eiffel Tower sparkles with thousands of lights, creating a magical atmosphere that attracts photographers and travelers from around the globe. Today, it remains one of the most visited monuments in the world.

Quick Facts:
  • Location: Paris, France
  • Built: 1887–1889
  • Height: 330 meters
  • Designer: Gustave Eiffel
